We think the big opportunity in the technology space right now is this idea of digital transformation. It's this notion that businesses really need to go on a digital journey or they run the risk of being disrupted by not only all the big digital disruptors we know about but a whole class of emerging digital disruptors. These companies are companies that are very comfortable using data to understand their customers, understand their business processes and do engage with customers and partners in a very software-centric way. So we believe that the companies that don't go on this type of journey that don't adopt more technology run the risk of being disrupted. So we think that's the big opportunity. We think it's going to drive spending in the category for the next 10 years easily. Some of the really interesting sub-themed are artificial intelligence and machine learning. Most data that businesses collect, they never go through, they never make any sense of it. So AI and machine learning really affords that opportunity. Cloud computing, massive opportunity aimed at helping businesses reach out and engage with their customers in a digital manner but with compute infrastructure wherever their customers are. So there's a number of very exciting sort of big digital cloud computing companies but then a number of very interesting smaller ones. We also have a high exposure to this idea of changing commerce. So obviously there's big themes around e-commerce and certainly there's big opportunities in FinTech. Along with e-commerce there's all sorts of new ways of paying, so interesting digital payments companies and all sorts of new data that comes as a result of collecting and engaging with customers in a digital way. Finally the internet of things, a very big opportunity to solve a lot of things. We think the opportunity though that when those things become connected a lot of data is going to come off those things. That data will help businesses better understand their customers and understand their business processes. So we found not only a number of exciting companies that are going to help build the things, semiconductor companies, connector companies but we really like some of the software and services companies. They're going to facilitate the management of internet of things and get data out of those things. It's ultimately all aimed at getting insights about customers and business processes. One key risk would be the current macro environment. Conditions look pretty good for enterprise spending right now. Consumer spending and tech has been a little more problematic with weakness in mobile phones but we're seeing good strength in many of the services that consumers are consuming on their mobile phones. And of course China has been slowing from a macro perspective for a while now. The fact is though that China doesn't consume a lot of global tech. Outside of semis which we think they spend roughly $150 billion annually on. They've got their own consumer internet companies, they've got their own networking companies. They've got their own hardware companies. They rely heavily on open source so the slowing economic activity we see in China isn't a major risk for global technology we believe. Our work with enterprise buyers and businesses indicates that even in the face of the slowing economic environment that the spending on digital transformation initiatives will remain relatively resilient.

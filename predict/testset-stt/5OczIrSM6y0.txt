Some of the main moves in markets in August mirror the key trends in markets since the beginning of the year. The use economy remains strong, supported by fiscal expansion and a patient-fed. In contrast, the Eurozone and emerging markets growth, while still robust, has weakened. As the prices have reflected these developments, and as a result, US equities have outperformed emerging market equities and European equities. The other salient move in markets has been renewed stress in emerging markets. In early August, stress in Turkey weaker than expected activity growth in China and a stronger US dollar led to weaker emerging markets across the board. In terms of asset allocation, our long-held view of being long equities and underweight fixed income, especially in the Eurozone, remains in place. But we have adjusted and reduced some of our risk exposure recently. In particular, we have made three key adjustments to our asset allocation. First, we have taken profits on our long UX banks position. Second, we have increased our underweight emigration taking advantage of very low German yields.

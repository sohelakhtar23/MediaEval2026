HSBC offers the global trade finance solutions that help you make the most of your working capital and trade with more confidence internationally. Our trade academy learning programme has been designed to demystify trade and help you navigate the world of trade finance. Every business wants to maximize its cash flow and reduce the time spent waiting for invoices to be paid. Receivables finance products could improve your time to cash and help protect against a buyer being unable to pay. Once you've delivered your goods or services and issued an invoice, HSBC pays up to 90% of the invoice value, normally within 24 hours. You receive the remaining balance, minus fees, once the buyer has paid the full amount. In the unlikely event that your buyer does not pay, you bear the risk of default, but you can manage that risk through your own trade credit insurance, or by taking up the option of making the facility limited recourse. Your buyer can be aware of the bank's role in the transaction, referred to as disclosed, or unaware, known as undisclosed. This disclosure can enable the bank to conduct collection of the receivables or ensure payment to the bank, relieving you of that burden. You can save time and reduce the risk of manual errors by uploading invoices to your receivables finance platform, such as HSBC Net. Within HSBC Net, you can also generate reports to track, manage and reconcile payments and see the funding that has been generated for your account. HSBC offers receivables finance products in 34 countries and territories around the world. Providing over $23 billion of funding to support domestic and export sales.

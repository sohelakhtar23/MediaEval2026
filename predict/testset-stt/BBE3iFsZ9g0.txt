Can sustainability accelerate recovery? There are four key areas for increased resilience. Our systems, policies, companies, and innovation. With regard to systems, we've heard clearly for many years the importance of focusing on climate, but also the broader societal system and inequalities in it remain salient conversations to be addressed as we think about how to strengthen for the future. The continued importance of thinking about not just companies, but also the communities with which companies interact and all those have a stake in what is going on remains critical. In this very unusual moment we've had because of COVID-19. We all know that there is unprecedented policy action taking place. We think that in Europe there could be significant opportunity in thinking about energy as new policies are formed and in the US and all over the world increase public private partnership. So as I move toward resilience in companies now that we've considered system and policy resilience, the importance of thinking about what companies are doing and also how companies are going about their business. One more point is resilience in innovation. The world had the experience in these last few months of having our processes completely offended, but true strength in our focus on innovation over so many years. Our work in sustainable investing has made it so that we are more aware of the world around us. That stance has given us strength to incorporate sustainability, but also to shift our way of thinking. And finally we have a focus on transition. Yes environmental transition, but how much our economies are changing and will change.

The PRI has launched an initiative called the inevitable policy response, which actually puts forward a forecast policy scenario for exactly what kinds of policies and changes will see put into place to help us transition. We pulled our audience today and the PRI did the same poll at their plenary event, asking, will we see a smooth and orderly transition or will it be disorderly? In each case, the majority of people said it will be disorderly. Now just because we think it will be disorderly doesn't mean we shouldn't try to forecast how it might unfold and that's exactly what the PRI have done with their new inevitable policy response work. And there are really kind of three headline takeaways for investors. The first is around the electrification of the energy system. They predict that by 2030, half of energy supply will be renewable. The second is around the kind of rapid change to transport that within 20 years, 70% of passenger vehicles will be electric. And the third is around changes to land use. They forecast the virtual elimination of deforestation by 2030, which is a very bold, ambitious, and very hopeful target. And I think an area that investors have focused less on in terms of how we can use our capital to support the end of deforestation. The issue is, however, that the basket of policies doesn't get us to 1.5 degrees. So investing for the near term, we're looking at this forecast, we're thinking about how our opportunity set and risks will change. But we're also leaning in as future makers to push for continued policy efforts to achieve the changes we need to see to successfully implement the Paris Agreement.

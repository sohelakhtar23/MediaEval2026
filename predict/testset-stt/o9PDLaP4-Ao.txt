Morning, my name is Bob Caner. I'm the head of US Small and Mid-Cat Equities here at Trotters. This morning, the US reported an annualized GDP of negative 32.9 percent. And while slightly better than muted expectations, it was however the worst number that we have seen since the Great Depression. Fortunately, GDP is a backward-looking metric. And what's most important for US Small and Mid-Cat Equities is the surging activity that we've seen in the months of May and June. And a continuation of that momentum as we enter the third quarter. That momentum has led to a plethora of earning surprises this season admittedly off of a low base. But looking forward, as the US economy continues to inflect off the bottom, we expect to see a broadening of growth, which will lead to positive earning surprises for US Small and Mid-Cat companies relative to their large cap peers. As the relative earnings growth improves, we expect to see a continued catch-up in performance in Small and Mid-Cat companies versus their large cap peers, which would be a continuation of the trend that we have seen since the market bottomed in late March.
